### Question 1

Two point charges $q_1 = -4.0\ \mu\text{C}$ and $q_2 = +4.0\ \mu\text{C}$ are lying on the $y$ axis as shown in Figure-1. They are equidistance from point P, which lies on the $x$ axis.

**(a)** (4 marks) Calculate the net electric field $\vec{E}$ at the point P.

**(b)** (3 marks) Calculate the net electric potential $V$ at the point P.

**(c)** (4 marks) If a small object of charge $q_o = +8.0\ \mu\text{C}$ is placed at point P, calculate the potential energy of the system.

> **Figure 1:** Two charges $q_1$ (positive, upper) and $q_2$ (positive, lower) each at 70 cm from point P on the $x$ axis. The lines from each charge to P make angles of 31° above and below the $x$ axis respectively.

### Question 2

The Figure-2 shows an electron entering the lower left side of a parallel plate capacitor and exiting at the upper right side. The initial speed of the electron is $v_0 = 7 \times 10^6\ \text{m/s}$. The capacitor is 2.0 cm long and its plates are separated by 0.15 cm. Assume that the electric field between the capacitor is uniform everywhere and the electron travels a vertical displacement $y = 0.15\ \text{cm}$ as it leaves the capacitor. Use the charge and mass of the electron: $q_e = -1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}$ and $m_e = 9.1 \times 10^{-31}\ \text{kg}$ respectively.

**(c)** (3 marks) If we place an electric dipole with dipole moment $\vec{p} = 6.2 \times 10^{-30}\ \hat{i}\ \text{C·m}$ inside the capacitor, calculate the torque on the dipole.

**(d)** (2 marks) How much work must an external agent do to rotate this dipole by 180°?
